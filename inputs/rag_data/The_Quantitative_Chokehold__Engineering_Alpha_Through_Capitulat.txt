Every day, the global equity markets process millions of trades. It is a highly chaotic environment where asset prices are violently pushed and pulled by new cycles, institutional algorithms, and pure retail panic. Surviving this environment requires eliminating human discretion. You extract consistent alpha, not by predicting the future, but by building a rigid mechanical architecture for equity selection and risk management. The math works by combining two opposing states. We are actively isolating companies currently reporting their highest historical fundamental performance, but we only acquire them when their stock price is in a state of severe technical capitulation. Executing this requires absolute parameters. Every single trade must have an exact predefined entry price, and a strict exit target locked in before capital is deployed. Emotional intervention is entirely removed. Intuition fails under pressure. To execute consistently, the investor must stop making discretionary bets, and instead operate a pulled statistical machine. The overarching methodology functions as a massive quantitative funnel. It is designed to aggressively filter down the universe of publicly traded equities into a highly restricted subset. The system does not attempt to predict which stocks will rise. Instead, it systematically deletes any asset that possesses a structural, financial, or statistical vulnerability. This filtration occurs in sequential layers. We first apply strict fundamental quality checks to verify the underlying business, and then overlay harsh technical distress triggers to ensure an asymmetric entry. It is a pure process of elimination. By stripping away overvalued, opaque, and correlated assets, the system narrows the search space to a specific group of companies that lack standard market vulnerabilities. The first layer of the funnel establishes the baseline business quality. We start with a strict size cutoff, eliminating any company with a market capitalization below 2,500 curl rupees to neutralize the elevated risk of book manipulation. Next, we apply a severe leverage constraint. For non-financial companies, the net debt to equity ratio must sit strictly below 0.2. This constraint is calculated by taking the gross debt and subtracting cash equivalents. This yields the true net debt, which is then mapped against equity and reserves to ensure it stays below the 0.2 threshold. The distinction between gross and net debt is critical. A company carrying high gross debt might still pass the filter if it holds massive cash reserves, proving the underlying balance sheet is structurally sound. We then enforce historical growth logic. Both the trailing 12-months net profit and total sales must be at or above 90% of the highest recorded print the company has achieved in the last 10 years. By demanding near-decade high profits, the system strictly limits capital allocation to proven, actively succeeding business models. Speculative investments are mathematically deleted from the board. There is a specific hurdle for aggressively expanding companies. If a stock's current price-to-earnings ratio exceeds its five-year median, standard models flag it as overvalued and rejected. The blueprint employs one specific exception. A stock with an elevated PE ratio remains viable only if its tangible fixed assets, like land, buildings, and machinery, are at or above 90% of their 10-year high. Heavy capital expenditure temporarily depresses short-term net profits due to upfront costs. That drop-in profit artificially spikes the PE ratio, making the company look expensive on paper. Tracking the expansion of land, buildings, and machinery allows the system to identify companies that are aggressively building out future production capacity. A pristine balance sheet is mathematically useless to this system unless the asset is experiencing severe technical distress. We must buy quality at a discount. Eligible stocks must trade strictly below their 200-day moving average. Anything above is discarded. Next, downfall from all-time high requires a steep discount. Large caps must collapse 30%, mid caps 40%, and small caps 60% to survive. We then apply a final verification filter. The stock's one-year and three-year price compound annual growth rate must sit lower than its compounded profit growth over the same periods. Demanding that the stock's price growth lags behind its profit growth while sitting deep inside a capitulation bracket isolates the exact statistical pressure point for asymmetric upside. The final layer of filters targets behavioral and structural hazards that math struggles to quantify. First, we permanently exclude vertical rally stocks to avoid brutal multi-year mean reversions. We strictly avoid all small and medium enterprises. These low liquidity assets are highly susceptible to operator-driven pump and dump schemes. Government-owned companies are also excluded due to a structural misalignment of motives. Their primary directive is public service, meaning generated profits are frequently siphoned off by the state rather than return to shareholders. Construction and infrastructure companies are entirely banned from the portfolio due to historically opaque accounting practices and highly unpredictable cash flows. Eliminating industries with built-in structural opacity and misaligned incentives is just as vital to the survival of the portfolio as identifying the perfect mathematical setup. Once the surviving subset of fully compliant equities, the list we call the Awesome 45 is isolated. Individual stock selection takes a backseat to the rigid rules of capital allocation. The portfolio maintains between 40 and 50 positions. Capital is strictly capped by size, large caps at 5%, mid caps at 3%, and small caps at 1.5%. Finally, no single sector can exceed 20% of the total allocation. This mechanical sizing grid neutralizes the threat of a single asset or single sector catastrophic drawdown. It prioritizes the long-term survival of the portfolio over any individual trade conviction. With the capital grid established, the final step before deployment is ranking the surviving fully compliant stocks to determine which assets receive capital first. This tracks the potential gain percent or PGP. It calculates yield by taking target price, subtracting entry price, dividing by entry price, and multiplying by 100. These precise prices are derived by overlapping technical strategies until a high probability zone is verified. The system dictates an absolute automation rule. Immediately upon purchasing a stock, a good-till triggered sell order is placed. The ultimate sell order is parked at exactly 99% of the target price. With the trigger sets slightly below at 98.5%. Executing just underneath the round-number target price ensures immediate liquidity fills. It entirely bypasses the human greed that inevitably causes traders to hold on for too long, missing the exit window. Once the capital is deployed and the GTT order is locked, all human intervention ceases. The system runs itself. The architecture demands exceptional fundamental strength, sources it during severe technical capitulation, and secures the execution inside an unbreakable algorithmic risk cage. The standard retail trading experience is driven by narrative, emotion, and discretionary panic during notional drawdowns. This framework removes those variables entirely. While the broader market panic is around it, the system operates with calm, systematic precision, unbothered by the external noise. The objective is to strip the romance and speculation out of equity trading. It forces the investor to evolve from a reactive gambler into a disciplined system operator, executing a series of high-probability statistical setups.
